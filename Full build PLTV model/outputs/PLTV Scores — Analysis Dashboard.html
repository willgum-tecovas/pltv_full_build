Distribution of remaining PLTV ($, clipped at p99 ≈ $1,274)

Heavily right-skewed: most customers cluster under ~$250, a long tail of high-value loyalists stretches out. Median $227 sits well below the mean, the signature of a concentrated book.

Value concentration by decile (% of total remaining $)

The top 10% of customers hold 29.8% of remaining value; the top 30% hold ~57%. Classic Pareto tilt — retention/CRM spend is best aimed at the top deciles.

Mean remaining PLTV by order stage (order_seq)

PLTV climbs steeply with each order placed — a 1st-order customer projects ~$181, a 7th-order customer ~$1,275, because repeat probability compounds. The 8+ bucket dips (~$998): it blends many long-tenure stages and the survival tail flattens.

Customers vs. value by risk tier

High-churn-risk customers are 35% of the scored base but only 14% of remaining value; Low-risk are 15% of customers yet 36% of value. The score is separating who's worth retaining.

Read of the book

Remaining (go-forward) PLTV totals $350M across 1.21M scored customers, and it is highly concentrated: value rises sharply with order stage (loyalty compounds the repeat rate), and the top decile alone carries ~30% of the total. The risk-tier split confirms the score is economically meaningful — Low-risk customers are a small slice of the base but the largest slice of value. The one thing to fix before circulating numbers: the 39% of customers with null AOV (mostly first-order) are excluded from every total here, so $350M understates the full book — re-running with a fallback AOV will fold them in and is the single highest-leverage correction.
